My Best 3 Ramen restaurants in Toronto

by Ikumi Mine, September 21st, 2020

There are so many ramen restaurants in Toronto nowadays. I also like eating ramen. Never get bored! In today’s article, I will introduce my favorite ramen restaurants in Toronto. My opinions might be biased, so please leave me some comments below if you know better places!

Ramen Isshin

I like their flavourful miso ramen because they make it by stir-frying some vegetables and soup together in the wok with miso and their special seasoning. This is perfect ramen when you want to eat really heavy ramen. They also have “Tsuke men” which is a dip style ramen. The soup is served in the hot stone bowl, it will get richer because the soup is being boiled in the hot stone. They have 3 spicy levels for their signature miso ramen and not spicy is $13.8, spicy is $15.8, and the spiciest(called red dragon) is $ 16.2.

Locations: 421 College St, 111 Richmond St W, 609 Queen St W

Hokkaido Ramen Santouka

This is the one of famous ramen restaurants in Japan. When I ate their ramen for the first time, I tasted like ramen that I ate in Japan. I have never eaten their ramen when I lived in Japan though. Their portion is not too much, so If you want to eat some flavourful ramen but not too heavy, this is a place to go. They have 4 different flavours for basic ramen which are Shio, Syoyu, and Miso and all of them are $14.00.

Locations: 91 Dundas St E, 2057 Yonge St, 515 Bloor St W

Hakata Shoryuken Ramen

The best thing at Hakata Shoryuken Ramen is the price! Although most ramen restaurants in Toronto is a little bit expensive, their price is reasonable. Hakata Tonkotsu ramen is $11.99. If you do not want to spend money too much but craving ramen, I recommend you to go to this place. Careful the restaurant is closed between 3:30 pm - 5:00 pm.

Locations: 225 Queen St W
